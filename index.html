CVPR 2015 Tutoral: Group Behavior Analysis and Its Applications

Description

In social scenes, people form multiple groups and their behaviors are governed by mutual interactions. Their behaviors are often strongly correlated, e.g., they pay attention to the same point of interest, walk together in a crowd, or make reciprocal gestures during dyadic interactions. Reliable determination of such group behaviors has many important applications, such as image retrieval, video editing, surveillance, and scene understanding.

In this tutorial, we will focus on the computational representation for group behavior analysis that reflects the latent structure of social interactions and discuss its applications to social scene understanding.
